Common questions answered

Everything you're
probably wondering.

What happens if my finance isn't approved in time?

Contact us immediately if you're running close to the deadline. We may be able to request an extension from the vendor. Don't wait until the last minute — the earlier we know, the more options we have.

If finance is formally declined, your contract may be terminated and your deposit returned, subject to the contract terms.

What if the building & pest report finds issues?

A building and pest report almost always finds something — that's normal. The question is whether the issues are significant enough to affect your decision or negotiate on price.

Share the report with your solicitor and discuss your options before the B&P period expires. You may be able to request repairs, a price reduction, or terminate the contract depending on the findings.

Do I need a solicitor or can I use a conveyancer?

Either is perfectly fine. A licensed conveyancer handles the legal transfer of property and is often the most cost-effective choice. A solicitor offers broader legal advice if your situation is more complex.

We've listed three firms we trust further down this page, or feel free to choose any Queensland-licensed practitioner of your choice.

When can I do a pre-settlement inspection?

Your pre-settlement inspection will typically take place the day before settlement. This is your opportunity to walk through the property and confirm it's in the same condition as when you purchased it and that all agreed inclusions are present.

Contact us to arrange a time — we'll coordinate access with the vendor.

When do I get the keys?

Keys are handed over on settlement day once funds have been confirmed as received. Your solicitor will notify you when settlement is complete — usually by 4pm. We'll be in touch as soon as we hear to arrange key collection or delivery.

What happens if something goes wrong before settlement?

Call us first. Whether it's a finance issue, a property concern, or a change in circumstances — there are usually options available. Our role doesn't end at signing; we're here all the way through to settlement.
